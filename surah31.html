Surah Luqman – Luqman

Classification: Makki | Verses: 34

Surah Luqman, revealed in Makkah, is the thirty-first chapter of the Quran. It emphasizes the wisdom of Luqman, a righteous man, who advised his son about faith, gratitude, and righteous conduct. The surah highlights the importance of avoiding shirk (associating partners with Allah), establishing prayer, showing humility, and practicing patience. It also reminds humanity of Allah’s signs in creation, urging reflection on the heavens, earth, and the balance of life. The chapter concludes with the reminder that ultimate knowledge of the unseen—such as the Hour, the wombs, and future provisions—belongs only to Allah.

  • Opens with the Quran as guidance and mercy for the believers.
  • Warns against those who distract from Allah’s message with falsehood and idle talk.
  • Mentions Luqman’s advice to his son: avoid shirk, establish prayer, be grateful, and live humbly.
  • Encourages good character: patience, humility, moderation in speech, and kindness to parents.
  • Calls to reflect on the signs of Allah in creation and nature.
  • Affirms that worldly attractions are temporary, while the Hereafter is everlasting.
  • Closes by declaring that knowledge of the unseen rests solely with Allah.
