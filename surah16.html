Surah An-Nahl – The Bee

Classification: Makki | Verses: 128

Surah An-Nahl, revealed in Makkah, is the sixteenth chapter of the Quran. It emphasizes Allah’s blessings in creation, including animals, plants, and natural resources, highlighting His mercy, wisdom, and power. The surah calls people to gratitude, obedience, and moral responsibility while warning against ingratitude, disbelief, and idolatry. It addresses the authenticity of the divine message and the importance of following guidance, offering examples from nature and past communities. Believers are encouraged to reflect, remain steadfast, and uphold justice and ethical conduct.

  • Highlights Allah’s blessings in creation, including animals, plants, and natural resources.
  • Encourages gratitude, obedience, and moral responsibility toward Allah and others.
  • Warns against disbelief, ingratitude, and idol worship.
  • Emphasizes the authenticity and guidance of the divine message.
  • Provides lessons from nature and previous communities as reminders for believers.
  • Encourages reflection, patience, and steadfastness in faith.
  • Stresses justice, ethical behavior, and reliance on Allah in all aspects of life.
