Surah Al-Haqqah – The Inevitable

Classification: Makki | Verses: 52

Surah Al-Haqqah, revealed in Makkah, focuses on the inevitability of the Day of Judgment and the consequences of human actions. The surah begins by describing the overwhelming reality of the “Inevitable” and the events of the Day of Judgment, emphasizing that denial and mockery of this truth will bring severe punishment. It recounts the fate of past nations such as the people of Pharaoh, Aad, and Thamud, who rejected their prophets and faced destruction. The chapter highlights the distinction between the righteous, who will receive rewards and bliss, and the disbelievers, who will endure torment. It calls people to reflect on their deeds, prepare for the Hereafter, and seek Allah’s mercy and guidance.

  • Describes the inevitability of the Day of Judgment and its overwhelming events.
  • Warns against denial, mockery, and heedlessness regarding the Hereafter.
  • Recounts the destruction of past nations who rejected divine guidance.
  • Contrasts the fate of the righteous with that of the disbelievers.
  • Encourages reflection, moral accountability, and preparation for the Hereafter.
  • Calls for seeking Allah’s mercy, guidance, and adherence to His commands.
  • Illustrates the certainty of divine justice and the consequences of human choices.
