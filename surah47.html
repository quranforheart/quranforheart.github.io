Surah Muhammad – The Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ)

Classification: Madani | Verses: 38

Surah Muhammad, revealed in Madinah, is the forty-seventh chapter of the Quran. It emphasizes the distinction between believers and hypocrites, highlighting the qualities of the true followers of Islam. The surah discusses the rewards for those who fight in the path of Allah with sincerity and faith, and the consequences for disbelievers. It provides guidance on obedience, trust in Allah, and maintaining unity within the Muslim community. The chapter also emphasizes that victory and ultimate success belong to Allah, and that human effort combined with divine guidance leads to true achievement.

  • Distinguishes between believers, who follow Allah’s guidance, and hypocrites or disbelievers, who oppose it.
  • Encourages believers to fight in the path of Allah with sincerity and steadfastness.
  • Highlights the spiritual and material rewards for those who struggle in Allah’s cause.
  • Warns disbelievers of punishment and the futility of their worldly attachments.
  • Emphasizes obedience to Allah and His Messenger as essential for guidance and success.
  • Encourages trust in Allah while actively striving for righteousness.
  • Highlights the importance of unity and loyalty within the Muslim community.
  • Reaffirms that ultimate victory and reward rest solely with Allah.
