Surah At-Tawbah – The Repentance

Classification: Madani | Verses: 129

Surah At-Tawbah, revealed in Madinah, is the ninth chapter of the Quran. It emphasizes repentance, accountability, and the responsibilities of the Muslim community, particularly in relation to treaties, alliances, and conflicts. The surah warns against hypocrisy, idolatry, and disobedience, while calling believers to sincerity, unity, and active support of Islam. It also addresses the conduct of war, financial obligations, and ethical treatment of both Muslims and non-Muslims, highlighting the importance of justice, courage, and faithfulness to divine guidance.

  • Emphasizes repentance (Tawbah) and returning to Allah with sincerity.
  • Warns against hypocrisy, idolatry, and betrayal of treaties.
  • Guides believers on maintaining unity, courage, and commitment to Islam.
  • Provides rules for conduct in warfare, alliances, and community defense.
  • Encourages fulfilling financial obligations and supporting the welfare of the Muslim community.
  • Highlights the ethical treatment of both Muslims and non-Muslims.
  • Reinforces accountability, moral integrity, and steadfastness in faith and action.
