Surah Ar-Ra'd – The Thunder

Classification: Madani | Verses: 43

Surah Ar-Ra’d, revealed in Madinah, is the thirteenth chapter of the Quran. It emphasizes the Oneness of Allah, the power of His creation, and His control over the universe. The surah highlights the truth of revelation and the consequences of belief and disbelief. It encourages reflection on natural phenomena, such as thunder, rain, and mountains, as signs of Allah’s wisdom and authority. The surah also calls believers to patience, steadfastness, and reliance on divine guidance while warning against arrogance and neglect of faith.

  • Highlights the Oneness of Allah and His supreme power over creation.
  • Encourages reflection on natural signs like thunder, rain, and mountains as evidence of Allah’s wisdom.
  • Emphasizes the truth of divine revelation and the consequences of belief and disbelief.
  • Warns against arrogance, stubbornness, and neglect of faith.
  • Encourages patience, steadfastness, and reliance on Allah in times of trial.
  • Stresses moral responsibility, accountability, and ethical living according to Allah’s guidance.
  • Reinforces the importance of recognizing and following divine guidance in all aspects of life.
