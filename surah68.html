Surah Al-Qalam – The Pen

Classification: Makki | Verses: 52

Surah Al-Qalam, revealed in Makkah, opens by swearing by the pen and what it writes, highlighting the importance of knowledge, truth, and moral responsibility. The surah addresses the character of the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ), emphasizing his patience, integrity, and unwavering commitment to guidance despite opposition. It warns those who mock or oppose the message, recounting the fate of previous communities who denied their prophets. The surah emphasizes the consequences of arrogance, greed, and disbelief, while encouraging steadfastness, ethical behavior, and trust in Allah’s plan. Through vivid examples and parables, it illustrates the contrast between the righteous and the corrupt, inspiring reflection, moral consciousness, and perseverance.

  • Swears by the pen, emphasizing knowledge, writing, and moral responsibility.
  • Highlights the Prophet’s character: patience, integrity, and steadfastness.
  • Warns mockers, deniers, and those who oppose the divine message.
  • Recounts the consequences faced by previous communities who rejected guidance.
  • Contrasts the righteous with the arrogant, greedy, and corrupt.
  • Encourages steadfastness, ethical conduct, and trust in Allah’s plan.
  • Inspires reflection, moral awareness, and perseverance in faith.
