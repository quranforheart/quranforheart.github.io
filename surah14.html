Surah Ibrahim – Prophet Abraham

Classification: Makki | Verses: 52

Surah Ibrahim, revealed in Makkah, is the fourteenth chapter of the Quran. It focuses on the message of Prophet Abraham (Ibrahim) and the importance of gratitude, faith, and trust in Allah. The surah emphasizes the consequences of accepting or rejecting divine guidance, the fate of previous nations, and the significance of sincere devotion. It also encourages believers to reflect on Allah’s signs, the blessings of revelation, and the rewards for patience and steadfastness.

  • Highlights the life and message of Prophet Abraham as a model of faith and devotion.
  • Emphasizes gratitude to Allah for His guidance and blessings.
  • Warns against disbelief, arrogance, and rejection of divine messages.
  • Encourages reflection on the experiences of past nations as lessons for believers.
  • Stresses the importance of patience, steadfastness, and reliance on Allah.
  • Highlights the significance of sincere worship and moral accountability.
  • Reminds believers of the rewards for following divine guidance and maintaining faith.
