Surah Al-Mumtahina – The Woman to be Examined

Classification: Madani | Verses: 13

Surah Al-Mumtahina, revealed in Madinah, provides guidance on interactions between Muslims and non-Muslims, particularly concerning allegiance, marriage, and loyalty. The name “The Woman to be Examined” refers to the procedure for testing the faith of women seeking to join the Muslim community. The surah emphasizes the importance of loyalty to Allah and His Messenger while balancing compassion and justice in dealings with others. It instructs believers not to form alliances with enemies of Islam, but also highlights the possibility of forgiveness and reconciliation when hostility ends. The chapter underscores ethical conduct, the protection of one’s community, and maintaining integrity in matters of faith and social relations.

  • Guides on testing the faith of women who wish to join the Muslim community.
  • Emphasizes loyalty to Allah and His Messenger over allegiance to non-believers in conflict.
  • Encourages fair treatment and ethical conduct toward those who are no longer hostile.
  • Addresses marriage and alliances with non-Muslims in the context of faith and morality.
  • Warns against supporting enemies of Islam while advocating forgiveness and reconciliation when hostility ends.
  • Stresses the importance of integrity, justice, and maintaining the strength of the community.
