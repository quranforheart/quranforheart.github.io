Surah Az-Zumar – The Groups

Classification: Makki | Verses: 75

Surah Az-Zumar, revealed in Makkah, is the thirty-ninth chapter of the Quran. It highlights the themes of sincerity in worship, the futility of associating partners with Allah, and the inevitability of the Day of Judgment. The surah contrasts the fate of the believers and disbelievers, stressing that deeds will be judged with perfect justice. It calls people to turn back to Allah in repentance before it is too late, and reminds them of His vast mercy as well as His severe punishment. The imagery of people entering Paradise in groups and others being driven to Hell in groups gives the surah its title, “The Groups.” The surah ends with a majestic scene of the angels surrounding Allah’s Throne, glorifying Him as justice is established.

  • Stresses the importance of sincerity (ikhlas) in worship and obedience to Allah alone.
  • Refutes polytheism and emphasizes that idols cannot intercede or benefit their worshippers.
  • Encourages repentance, promising Allah’s forgiveness for all sins to those who return to Him sincerely.
  • Warns of the fate of those who deny the truth and are consumed by pride and arrogance.
  • Highlights the contrasting destinies of believers and disbelievers on the Day of Judgment.
  • Depicts the scene of resurrection, where groups of people will be led either to Hell or to Paradise.
  • Concludes with the vision of the angels glorifying Allah around His Throne, affirming divine justice and majesty.
